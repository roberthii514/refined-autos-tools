SEO guide for refined.autos

The exact steps to follow before and after publishing every article.

Keyword qualification — what to check in Mangools
✓
Search volume ≥ 100/month
Below 100 means almost nobody searches for it. Even ranking #1 gets you 5–20 visits/month — not worth the effort. If a keyword has 50 searches, find a broader version of it.
✓
KD (Keyword Difficulty) ≤ 39
KD 0–25 is ideal for a new site. KD 26–39 is acceptable. KD 40+ means established high-DA sites dominate — you cannot outrank them yet. When a keyword is too hard, search for a longer-tail version with a more specific modifier ("for sale under $20k", "2019 model", "vs [competitor]").
✓
At least 2–3 results in top 10 with DA under 30
This is the most important signal. If the entire top 10 is Car and Driver (DA 80+), Edmunds (DA 75+), and Motor Trend (DA 72+), you cannot break in. But if you see a random blog or forum at DA 18 ranking on page 1, that tells you Google is willing to rank smaller sites for this keyword.
!
Check the search intent by Googling the keyword yourself
Are the top results articles or videos? If top results are all YouTube videos, Google prefers video for that keyword — skip it. You want keywords where the top 10 are blog articles and guides, which is where your content will compete.
!
Use long-tail variations to find lower KD opportunities
If "BMW M3 review" has KD 60, try "BMW M3 review worth buying", "BMW M3 F80 review", or "BMW M3 daily driver review" in Mangools. Long-tail variations often have KD 10–25 with meaningful volume. These are your best early opportunities as a new site.
Before publishing — WordPress checklist
✓
Install Rank Math (free WordPress plugin)
Handles meta descriptions, XML sitemap, and on-page SEO scoring. Aim for a Rank Math score of 70+ before publishing. Install once, use forever.
✓
Set font size to 18–20px and line height to 1.6
Direct Journey by Mediavine recommendation. Larger text creates more vertical page height, which means more ad placement opportunities and higher RPM. Add to your WordPress theme CSS: body { font-size: 18px; line-height: 1.6; }
✓
Replace all [IMAGE:] placeholders with real images
Use free licensed images from Unsplash.com or Pexels.com. Set the alt text to your primary keyword followed by a short description. Images keep readers engaged longer — which directly increases RPM.
✓
Add 3–5 internal links to other articles on your site
Link from every new article to 3–5 older articles, and update older articles to link to new ones. This passes authority between pages and helps Google understand your site structure. It's free and highly effective.
✓
Submit sitemap to Google Search Console
Go to search.google.com/search-console, add your site, submit sitemap.xml. Do this the day you publish your first article. Without it, Google may take weeks to find your content.
!
Content container width: 728px–1024px maximum
Per Mediavine guidelines. Too-wide content is harder to read and reduces ad performance. Check your WordPress theme settings and cap the content area at 1024px.
Applying for Journey by Mediavine

Apply at journeybymediavine.com once you hit 1,000 sessions in any 30-day period.

• Remove any other ad network (AdSense etc.) before applying
• Have a Privacy Policy page on your site
• All content must be original — no duplicates or thin articles
• Check your 30-day sessions in Google Analytics or Search Console
